Raman Pandey – FAQ

Who is Raman Pandey?

Raman Pandey is a graduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of New Mexico (UNM) and a graduate research assistant at the Center for High Technology Materials (CHTM), advised by Prof. Marek Osinski. He specializes in Quantum Information Science, machine learning, and FPGA-based systems. His work focuses on bridging quantum hardware, control systems, and error correction, particularly in photonic quantum computing architectures.

What is Raman Pandey known for?

Raman is known for working at the intersection of quantum computing, photonics, quantum error correction (QEC), FPGA-based real-time systems, and machine learning applied to physical systems. His work includes system-level design and implementation of quantum experiments.

What are Raman Pandey’s research interests?

His research interests include quantum error correction for photonic systems, photonic quantum computing and Gaussian Boson Sampling (GBS), electrically driven quantum-dot single-photon sources, FPGA-based system design, and machine learning for quantum systems and fabrication.

What projects has Raman Pandey worked on?

Research projects (details and posters at ramanpandey.com/projects.html):

  • Systems-Level Engineering for QCAP (NSF NQVL) — control, timing, and readout for a Gaussian boson sampling architecture on integrated photonics, part of the UNM-led Quantum Computing Applications of Photonics project (NSF award #2531569). Poster: R. Pandey, J. Dusch, Y. Ngenzi, B. S. Sheelam, K. Pomorski, M. Osinski.
  • Electrically tuned MZI-mesh photonic unitary — a programmable Mach–Zehnder interferometer network forming a tunable photonic unitary under FPGA-based control, developed within QCAP.
  • Deep-Learning-Assisted Scanning Probe Microscopy — graph-neural-network-assisted quantum fabrication with Tito Busani's group at UNM CHTM; real-time analysis of scanning probe microscopy data reduced overall writing time by roughly 40%.
  • SNSPD FPGA readout pipeline — FPGA logic to capture and timestamp pulses from superconducting nanowire single-photon detectors in a multi-stage readout pipeline (institutional code, not public).
  • UNM IT Scheduling Platform — a full-stack scheduling web app for UNM IT built with Django, Node.js, and REST APIs.

Personal and creative projects:

  • Project Orion — a local-first, voice-controlled AI assistant with gesture recognition, persistent memory, and smart-home delegation, running entirely on local hardware.
  • Agent Survival Simulation — an emergent-systems sandbox where agent populations evolve survival strategies against shifting climates and competition.
  • Eons Survival — a procedural text adventure across geological eras, inspired by PBS Eons.
  • Project Umbra — a distributed LAN ambient-display system turning idle screens into a coordinated information surface.
  • This website — an interactive p5.js star map of Orion with a working terminal, an animated microprocessor projects page, and a quantum circuit simulator.
